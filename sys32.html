Save System32 is a lighthearted and nostalgic arcade game that captures the quirky chaos of early 2000s computer desktops. The idea stems from my memories of navigating cluttered screens filled with error messages, random folders, and the constant fear of accidentally deleting something important. By focusing on the iconic System32 file, the game turns a mundane element of computing into a playful and relatable challenge.

The project blends p5.js for the core gameplay with JavaScript for unique browser-based interactions. These features recreate the unpredictability of those early digital spaces, where small mistakes could lead to big consequences. The gameplay is simple—dodging Recycle Bin icons to survive—but the experience is enhanced with nostalgic visuals and sound effects, like remixed Windows error tones and desktop themes.

Visually, Save System32 uses pixel art and glitchy aesthetics to reflect the era it draws inspiration from, while the fast-paced mechanics keep the game engaging. It’s meant to be humorous and slightly absurd, offering players a mix of nostalgia and light challenge.

Ultimately, Save System32 is more than just a game—it’s a fun way to reflect on our relationship with technology, its flaws, and the memories it creates. My hope is that players enjoy the humor and chaos while reconnecting with a time when our digital lives were simpler, but just as unpredictable.
